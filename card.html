Stingray

Stingrays are a group of rays, which are cartilaginous fish related to sharks.

  • Scientific Name: Dasyatis pastinaca
  • Measure: length around 35 cm (14 in)
  • Living species: coastal tropical and subtropical marine waters throughout the world.
    •

Most stingrays have one or more barbed stingers (modified from dermal denticles) on the tail, which are used exclusively in self-defense.
